Twin Prime Conjecture

Are there infinitely many primes \(p\) such that \(p+2\) is also prime.

Proof to the Twin Prime Conjecture

Definitions

Let \(p_n\) be the nth prime number.

Let \(P_n\) be the multiplication of the first nth prime numbers.

Structure of Proof

First Part

\(a\) in \(A_n\) where \(a < P_n\) and \(a\) relatively prime to \(P_n\)

There always exist two numbers which differ by 2 in each subsequent set \(A_n\) where \(n \geq 3\).

Second Part

There always exist two prime numbers which differ by d in each subsequent nth set of arithmetic progressions: \(\{mP_n+a\}\) where \(n \geq 2\) and \(a\) in \(A_n\) and \(a < P_n\) and \(a\) relatively prime to \(P_n\) and \(0 \leq m < P_n\)

Creating set of pairs \(\{(m_1P_n+a_1,m_2P_n+a_2)\}\) where \(m_1=m_2\) and \(a_1+d=a_2\) and \(m_1P_n+a_1\) in \(\{mP_n +a_1\}\) and \(m_2P_n+a_1\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) and \(0 \leq m < P_n\)

Therefore there are infinite number of primes \(p\) where \(p+2\) is alos prime.

First part:

\(a\) in \(A_n\) where \(a < P_n\) and \(a\) relatively prime to \(P_n\)

There always exist two numbers which differ by 2 in each subsequent set \(A_n\) where \(n \geq 3\).

Base Case

The numbers \(11\) and \(13\) in \(A_3\)

Induction Case

A pair of numbers \((a_1,a_2)\) where \(a_1,a_2\) in \(A_n\) which differ by \(d\) and \(d>0\) and \(a_1+d=a_2\) will propagate at least \(p_{n+1}-2\) pairs of numbers \((b_1,b_2)\) where \(b_1,b_2\) in \(A_{n+1}\) which differ by \(d\) and \(b_1+d=b_2\)

\(a\) in \(A_n\) generates \(p_{n+1}-1\) elements in \(A_{n+1}\) via arithmetic progression: \(\{mP_n+a\}\) where \(0 \leq m < p_{n+1}\)

Unique value \(m_1P_n+a_1\) divisible by \(p_{n+1}\) in \(\{mP_n + a\}\) where \(0 \leq m < p_{n+1}\)

The unique value \(m_1P_n+a_1\) divisible by \(p_{n+1}\) in \(\{mP_n + a_1\}\) where \(0 \leq m < p_{n+1}\) and the unique value \(m_2P_n+a_2\) divisible by \(p_{n+1}\)in \(\{mP_n + a_2\}\) where \(0 \leq m < p_{n+1}\) has two possible cases \(m_1 = m_2\) and \(m_1 \neq m_2\)

If \(m_1 = m_2\)
Then a pair of numbers \((a_1,a_2)\) where \(a_1,a_2\) in \(A_n\) which differ by \(d\) and \(d>0\) and \(a_1+d=a_2\) will propagate \(p_{n+1}-1\) pairs of numbers \((b_1,b_2)\) where \(b_1,b_2\) in \(A_{n+1}\) which differ by \(d\) where \(b_1+d=b_2\)

If \(m_1 \neq m_2\)
Then a pair of numbers \((a_1,a_2)\) where \(a_1,a_2\) in \(A_n\) which differ by \(d\) and \(d>0\) and \(a_1+d=a_2\) will propagate \(p_{n+1}-2\) pairs of numbers \((b_1,b_2)\) where \(b_1,b_2\) in \(A_{n+1}\) which differ by \(d\) where \(b_1+d=b_2\)

Second part:

There always exist two prime numbers which differ by d in each subsequent nth set of arithmetic progressions: \(\{mP_n+a\}\) where \(n \geq 2\) and \(a\) in \(A_n\) and \(a < P_n\) and \(a\) relatively prime to \(P_n\) and \(0 \leq m < P_n\)

Creating set of pairs \(\{(m_1P_n+a_1,m_2P_n+a_2)\}\) where \(m_1=m_2\) and \(a_1+d=a_2\) and \(m_1P_n+a_1\) in \(\{mP_n +a_1\}\) and \(m_2P_n+a_1\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) and \(0 \leq m < P_n\)

If \(c\) in \(\{mP_n+a\}\) is a composite where \(0 \leq m < P_n\) then \(c = xo\) where \(x > 0\) and \(o\) is odd and \(3 \leq o \leq (P_n-1)\) because the largest value in \(\{mP_n+a\}\) is \((P_n-1)P_n+P_n-1=(P_n-1)(P_n+1)\)

Induction:
All composites in \(\{mP_n+a_1\}\) must be divisible by an odd number greater than or equal to \(3\) and less than or equal to \(P_n-1\).
All composites in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) must be divisible by an odd number greater than or equal to \(3\) and less than or equal to \(P_n-1\).
If there exist \(m_1P_n+a_1\) in \(\{mP_n+a_1\}\) such that \(m_1P_n+a_1\) is divisible by \(f\)
Then this is the set \(\{(m_1+xf)P_n+a_1\}\) of all elements divisible by \(f\) in \(\{mP_n+a_1\}\)
If there exist \(m_2P_n+a_2\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) such that \(m_2P_n+a_2\) is divisible by \(f\)
Then this is the set \(\{(m_2+xf)P_n+a_2\}\) of all elements divisible by \(f\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\)
Take \(P_n-1\) pairs of numbers \(\{(q_1,q_2)\}\) where \(q_1\) in \(\{mP_n +a_1\}\) and \(q_2\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) where \((x) \leq m < (x+P_n-1)\)
Assume there is unique \(m_1P_n+a_1\) divisible by \((P_n-1)\) in \(\{mP_n+a_1\}\).
Assume there is unique \(m_2P_n+a_2\) divisible by \((P_n-1)\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\).
Leaving \(P_n-1-2\) pairs of numbers \(\{(q_1,q_2)\}\) not divisible by \(P_n-1\) where \(q_1\) in \(\{mP_n +a_1\}\) and \(q_2\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) where \((x) \leq m < (x+P_n-1)\)
Assume the remaining \(P_n-1-2\) pairs of numbers \(\{(q_1,q_2)\}\) not divisible by \(P_n-1\) are sequential.
Therefore there exist \(P_n-1-2\) pairs of numbers \(\{(q_1,q_2)\}\) where \(q_1\) in \(\{mP_n +a_1\}\) and \(q_2\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) where \((x) \leq m < (x+P_n-1-2)\).

Leaving \(P_n-1-2-2\) pairs of numbers \(\{(q_1,q_2)\}\) not divisible by \(P_n-1-2\) where \(q_1\) in \(\{mP_n +a_1\}\) and \(q_2\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) where \((x) \leq m < (x+P_n-1-2)\)
Assume the remaining \(P_n-1-2-2\) pairs of numbers \(\{(q_1,q_2)\}\) not divisible by \(P_n-1-2\) are sequential.
Therefore there exist \(P_n-1-2-2\) pairs of numbers \(\{(q_1,q_2)\}\) where \(q_1\) in \(\{mP_n +a_1\}\) and \(q_2\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) where \((x) \leq m < (x+P_n-1-2-2)\).

\(\dots\)
Leaving \(1\) pair of numbers \(\{(q_1,q_2)\}\) not divisible by \(3\) where \(q_1\) in \(\{mP_n +a_1\}\) and \(q_2\) in \(\{mP_n+a_2\}\) where \((x) \leq m < (x+1)\). Therefore \(q_1,q_2\) must be both prime numbers.

Arithmetic Progressions

Let \(p_n\) be the nth prime number.
For example \(p_1=2,p_2=3,p_3=5\)

Let \(P_n\) be the multiplication of the first \(n\) prime numbers.
For example \(P_1 = p_1, P_2 = p_1 \times p_2, P_3 = p_1 \times p_2 \times p_3\)

Let \(a\) in \(A_n\) where a is relatively prime to \(P_n\) and less than \(P_n\).
For example
\(A_1=\{1\},A_2=\{1,5\},A_3=\{1,7,11,13,17,19,23,29\}\)

The arithmetic progressions

\(\{mP_n + a\}\)
